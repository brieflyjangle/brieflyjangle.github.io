Music Notes

February 11, 2026 Music 本文总阅读量次

A note is the most basic unit of music — a single sound with a specific pitch.

There are 12 unique notes in Western music:

C - C# - D - D# - E - F - F# - G - G# - A - A# - B

These 12 notes repeat in higher and lower octaves. Moving from one C to the next C above it covers all 12 notes — this is called a chromatic scale.

Key Points

  • A note has pitch (how high or low) and duration (how long it lasts)
  • Sharps (#) raise a note by a half step, flats (b) lower it by a half step
  • C# and Db are the same pitch — this is called an enharmonic equivalent

Reading Notes on the Staff

Music notes are written on a staff — a set of 5 lines and 4 spaces. Each line and space represents a different note. Which note depends on the clef at the beginning of the staff.

Treble Clef (G Clef)

The treble clef is used for higher-pitched instruments and the right hand on piano.

Lines (bottom to top):

Line Note Mnemonic
1st E Every
2nd G Good
3rd B Boy
4th D Does
5th F Fine

[!tip] Remember: Every Good Boy Does Fine

Spaces (bottom to top):

Space Note
1st F
2nd A
3rd C
4th E

[!tip] Remember: The spaces spell F-A-C-E

Bass Clef (F Clef)

The bass clef is used for lower-pitched instruments and the left hand on piano.

Lines (bottom to top):

Line Note Mnemonic
1st G Good
2nd B Boys
3rd D Do
4th F Fine
5th A Always

[!tip] Remember: Good Boys Do Fine Always

Spaces (bottom to top):

Space Note Mnemonic
1st A All
2nd C Cows
3rd E Eat
4th G Grass

[!tip] Remember: All Cows Eat Grass

Middle C — The Bridge

Middle C connects the treble and bass clefs. It sits on a small ledger line:

  • One ledger line below the treble clef staff
  • One ledger line above the bass clef staff

[!note] Ledger Lines
Notes that go beyond the 5 lines of the staff use short extra lines called ledger lines. Middle C is the most common example.

Note Duration

Notes also tell you how long to play:

Symbol Name Duration
𝅝 Whole note 4 beats
𝅗𝅥 Half note 2 beats
♩ Quarter note 1 beat
♪ Eighth note 1/2 beat
𝅘𝅥𝅯 Sixteenth note 1/4 beat

[!info] Each note value is half the duration of the one above it.

Related Concepts

  • The distance between two notes is called an Interval
  • When you stack multiple notes together using intervals, you get a Chord
